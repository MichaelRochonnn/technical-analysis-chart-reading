# Patterns And Price Structure

Read structure before naming patterns. A pattern is valid only inside a prior context.

## Trend

- Uptrend: higher highs and higher lows, preferably with rising participation and price above rising medium-term averages.
- Downtrend: lower highs and lower lows, preferably with rising participation on declines and price below falling medium-term averages.
- Sideways/range: overlapping swings, flat averages, repeated support/resistance tests, and oscillator usefulness increases.
- Multi-timeframe rule: higher timeframe trend sets the default bias; lower timeframe signals need extra confirmation when countertrend.

## Support And Resistance

- Mark recent swing highs/lows, gap edges, breakout levels, high-volume nodes if visible, and round/psychological levels.
- Treat levels as zones, not single ticks.
- Watch polarity: broken resistance can become support; broken support can become resistance.
- A valid breakout is stronger when followed by acceptance, retest success, volume expansion, or channel continuation.

## Trendlines And Channels

- Use at least two meaningful pivots; three touches strengthen confidence.
- A trendline break is a warning, not automatically a reversal.
- Channel overshoot can signal acceleration or exhaustion; decide by follow-through and participation.
- A failed return to the old channel after a break can confirm trend change.

## Gaps

- Common gap: appears inside congestion; often less meaningful.
- Breakaway gap: leaves a base or top; more important if volume expands.
- Runaway/measuring gap: appears mid-trend and may help estimate distance.
- Exhaustion gap: appears late after an extended move and requires reversal confirmation.

## Major Reversal Patterns

Use reversal labels only after an existing trend has matured.

- Head and shoulders: left shoulder, head, right shoulder, neckline. Confirmation requires neckline break; volume ideally weakens into the head/right shoulder and expands on break.
- Inverse head and shoulders: same structure inverted; watch neckline breakout and retest.
- Double top/bottom: not confirmed at the second test; confirm only after the middle trough/peak breaks.
- Triple top/bottom: repeated failure at a zone; confirm with range break.
- Rounding top/bottom: slow transition; requires patience and participation evidence.

## Continuation Patterns

- Triangle: compression. Direction depends on breakout and trend context, not the name alone.
- Ascending/descending triangle: horizontal boundary plus rising/falling opposite side; watch for false breaks.
- Flag/pennant: short pause after sharp impulse; should not last too long or retrace too deeply.
- Rectangle: range continuation or reversal depending on breakout direction.
- Wedge: contraction with slope; rising wedge often warns in an uptrend, falling wedge often warns in a downtrend, but context and break matter.

## Point-And-Figure

- Use when the goal is to filter time/noise and focus on price reversals.
- Define box size and reversal amount before analysis.
- Three-box reversal is a common default.
- Count targets are estimates; confirm with line/candle structure before trading.

## Elliott, Fibonacci, And Cycles

- Elliott wave counts are hypotheses. Keep alternate counts when the structure is ambiguous.
- Fibonacci retracements/extensions are zones of interest, not laws.
- Cycles and seasonality add timing context only when the phase is plausible and supported by current price action.

## Pattern Quality Checklist

- Prior trend exists.
- Pattern boundaries are visible and not forced.
- Volume/OI/participation behavior is consistent.
- Breakout or breakdown is confirmed by close, retest, or follow-through.
- Measured target is realistic relative to higher-timeframe support/resistance.
- Invalidation is clear.
