# Indicator Playbook

Use indicators after price structure. Indicators are evidence, not commands.

## Trend Tools

| Tool | Best use | Bullish evidence | Bearish evidence | Common failure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SMA/EMA | Trend direction, dynamic support/resistance | Close above rising MA; faster MA above slower MA | Close below falling MA; faster MA below slower MA | Whipsaws in ranges |
| MA crossover | Systematic trend following | Fast MA crosses above slow MA after base/breakout | Fast MA crosses below slow MA after distribution/breakdown | Late entries after extended moves |
| Price channel / Donchian | Breakout and trend following | Close above recent channel high | Close below recent channel low | False breakout in low-volume range |
| ATR | Volatility and stop distance | Expanding ATR confirms impulse or risk expansion | Contracting ATR warns of compression or fading impulse | ATR is directionless |

## Momentum And Oscillators

| Tool | Best use | Signal | Confirmation | Warning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Momentum / ROC | Speed of move | Rising above zero or improving from extreme | Price breaks structure in same direction | Divergence against new price extreme |
| RSI | Range extremes, trend health, divergence | In uptrend, RSI holds higher range and recovers from pullback | Higher low in RSI plus price support | Overbought can persist in strong uptrend |
| Stochastic | Range trading and turns near range edges | %K turns up through %D from low zone near support | Price holds support or breaks minor downtrend | Repeated false turns in strong trends |
| MACD | Trend/momentum blend | MACD above signal/zero; histogram improving | Price structure confirms breakout | Signal often late; divergence needs trigger |

## Volume

- Rising price with rising volume: participation supports the advance.
- Rising price with falling volume: advance may be vulnerable, especially near resistance.
- Falling price with rising volume: selling pressure supports the decline.
- Falling price with falling volume: decline may be losing force, but still needs price confirmation.
- Breakouts should ideally expand volume. Failed breakouts on heavy volume deserve attention.
- OBV should broadly confirm price trend. OBV divergence is an early warning, not a standalone reversal.

## Open Interest For Futures

Use open interest only where it is meaningful, usually futures and options.

| Price | Open interest | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Up | Up | New money supports the uptrend |
| Up | Down | Short covering or liquidation; advance may be less durable |
| Down | Up | New shorts support the downtrend |
| Down | Down | Long liquidation; decline may be less durable |

## Divergence Rules

1. Require a visible price extreme and a non-confirming indicator extreme.
2. Classify divergence as a warning first.
3. Demand a trigger: trendline break, support/resistance break, failed retest, MA/channel violation, or reversal pattern completion.
4. Prefer divergence on higher timeframes or across multiple indicators.
5. Do not fight a strong trend solely because an oscillator is overbought or oversold.

## Indicator Stack

Use a small stack that covers different information:

- Trend: 20/50/200 MA or channel.
- Momentum: RSI, MACD, ROC, or stochastic.
- Participation: volume, OBV, open interest if relevant.
- Volatility/risk: ATR and recent range.

If two indicators measure the same thing, treat them as one clue, not independent confirmation.
